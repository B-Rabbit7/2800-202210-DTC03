CO2 prouction per person

The average human produces 5 tonnes of CO2 each year. However, emissions in USA and Canada stand at 16 tonnes per person, triple the global average.

Despite China accounting for 27% of global CO2 emmisions, it also accounts for 18.5% of the worlds population. For this reason China's CO2 production per person is only 7 tons, less then half of what the United States or Canada produces.

Income Effects

The high and upper middle class make up a large majority of CO2 emissions, with a statistic of 3.8 billion people causing 86% of global emissions.

While 3.7 billion people making up the lower middle and low income bracket contributing to only 14% of global CO2 emissions.

Countries that contribute the lowest amount to CO2 emissions have the most to loose from climate change. Results include, large numbers of natural disasters, droughts, flooding, and scarcity of food due to crops and livestock damage.
